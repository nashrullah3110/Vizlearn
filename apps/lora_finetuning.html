Interactive Explainer: LoRA Fine-tuning

What is LoRA?

Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) is an efficient fine-tuning technique that adapts large pre-trained models without modifying their original weights.

Instead of updating the massive frozen weights (W), LoRA introduces two small matrices (A & B) that represent the adaptation as a low-rank decomposition.

The key insight: W' = W + B × A

Adapted Model (W') - Not Ready

Key Benefits

• Parameter Efficient: Only train ~0.1% of parameters
• Memory Efficient: Keep original model frozen
• Modular: Easy to swap different adaptations
• Fast Training: Reduced computational overhead
